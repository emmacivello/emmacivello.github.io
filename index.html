About

Hi, I'm Emma Civello. I'm a recent graduate of Smith College, where I double-majored in Computer Science and English Language & Literature. I am currently looking for programming positions, and I am especially interested in UI/UX programming, computer graphics, and video game development.

At Smith, I contributed to graph theory projects in Professor Ileana Streinu's lab. I programmed Mathematica prototypes, a web app, and most recently, a complete redesign of the lab website. I was also a developer on the Mothitor (moth-monitor) project, and I designed and helped assemble a multi-GPU workstation for the CS department's machine learning projects. Finally, I organized and analyzed ancient and modern sources for the virtual recreation of the Porticus of Livia, a project with Professor Barbara Kellum and the Smith Imaging Center.

Outside of programming, I enjoy reading science fiction and playing cello and piano.
